### Cisco IT Essentials – Theory Questions on Computer Components

1. Explain the role of the CPU in a computer system.

2. What is the difference between RAM and permanent storage?

3. Why is the motherboard considered the backbone of a computer?

4. Describe how a power supply unit (PSU) works.

5. What would happen if a computer had no RAM installed?

6. Explain the purpose of a heat sink and cooling fan.

7. Compare an SSD and an HDD.

8. What is BIOS/UEFI, and why is it important?

9. Explain the function of a graphics card.

10. What is the purpose of a network interface card (NIC)?

11. How does the CPU communicate with other components in a computer?

12. Why is electrical safety important when working inside a computer?

13. What is electrostatic discharge (ESD), and how can it damage components?

14. Why should a technician disconnect power before opening a computer case?

15. Explain the difference between input devices and output devices.

16. What are expansion slots, and why are they used?

17. Describe the process that occurs when a computer is powered on.

18. What is the relationship between the CPU, RAM, and storage devices?

19. Why do modern computers require cooling systems?

20. How does a motherboard support communication between hardware devices?

21. Explain why a PSU must provide the correct voltage to components.

22. What factors should be considered when selecting RAM for a computer?

23. How can a faulty PSU affect a computer's performance?

24. Why is compatibility important when upgrading computer components?

25. Describe the main components involved in storing, processing, and transmitting data within a computer.

26. What are the consequences of overheating in a computer system?

27. Explain the difference between hardware and software.

28. Why is firmware important in a computer?

29. Describe the role of storage devices during the boot process.

30. If a computer powers on but does not display anything on the screen, which components would you investigate first, and why?

These are the kind of theory questions that Cisco instructors often use for quizzes, assignments, and exams. Try answering them in your own words without looking at notes.
